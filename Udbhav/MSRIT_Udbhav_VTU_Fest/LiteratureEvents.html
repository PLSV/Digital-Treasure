LITERARY EVENTS RULES

  1. Debate(English) (Inter-College Event)

    Rules :
    • Each team will be represented by 2 participants; one will speak FOR, while another will speak AGAINST the motion.
    • Medium of the debate will be English.
    • Topics of the debate will be announced 24 hours in advance.
    • Each speaker will be allowed to speak for maximum five minutes.
    • Each Speaker will be asked one question by the judges in the rebuttal round at the end of the speech.
    • Paper reading is not allowed.


  2. Dumb Charades (Inter-College Event)

    Rules :
    • There MUST be three contestants in a team.
    • Contestants may not change/shuffle teams after the prelims.
    • Number/nature of rounds is up to the DC master.
    • Contestants may not speak; they may use only established codes, no dropping unless specified. More rules will be specified on-spot by the DC master.
    • Contestants may not use vulgar forms of expressions in the event.


  3. Creative Writing

    Rules :
    • Contestants are allowed to participate alone only.
    • Medium of expression will be English, Hindi or Kannada.
    • The topic/ theme will be announced at the start of the event.
    • Use of cell phone/computer/reading material will not be permitted once the event commences.
    • There will be no limit on the number of words.
    • Content can be in the form of essays, short stories or poetry.
    • There will be a time limit of 3 hours.


  4. General Quiz (Inter-College Event)

    Rules :
    • There will be a written preliminary round and teams will be selected for the final.
    • A team should consist of maximum of 3 people.
    • Finals will be oral with Audio-Visual questions.
    • The specific rules regarding evaluation procedure, time to reply a particular answer and the type of round will be given before the actual start of the competition.


  5. Spent Quiz

    Rules :
    • There will be written preliminary round and the teams will be selected for finals.
    • A team should consist of 3 people
    • Finals will be oral with audio-Visual questions.
    • The specific rules regarding evaluation procedure, time to reply a particular answer and the type of round will be given before the actual start of the competition
    • Questions will be pertaining to sports and entertainment only.


  6. Elocution

    Rules :
    • Medium of expression will be English.
    • Each speaker will be allowed to speak for maximum 5+2 minutes.
    • Subject/Topic of elocution will be announced one day prior to the event.
