FUN EVENTS RULES

  1. Personality

    Prelims :
    • Questionnaire to be collected from Pit Stop.
    • Form has to be submitted by the specified date and time.
    • You have to be creative, humorous and make sense.
    • No profanity is allowed.


    Finals :
    • Finalists will be announced on the morning of the day of the event.
    • All rounds will be announced by the coordinators/judges during the course of the finals.
    • Decision of the judges is final and binding.
    • No profanity is allowed.


  2. MSRIT’s Got Talent

    Rules :
    • It is an individual or a group of participants of maximum 5 people event, which showcases the talents of performers in music, dance, acting, art, or any other creative performance. (In case of more than one participant for an act the participants need to inform the event coordinator during registration).
    • It is strongly recommended that an act runs no more than 3 minutes. Under no circumstances may an act run more than 3 minutes 15 seconds. Acts exceeding time limit will be disqualified.
    • The act is timed from the first initiation of the act.
    • The participants must report at least an hour before the start of the event at the venue.
    • CD player will be provided. Events where music is required the participants need to submit the CD’s by the specified date at the PITSTOP. Please label your CD’s with name and USN for identification.
    • No dangerous props like fire, glass, etc can be used during any of the performances.
    • No props will be provided.
    • There will be two rounds in the event. All contestants must perform a different piece for finals than the one they performed for the preliminary show.
    • No vulgarity is allowed.


  3. On the Spot Photography (Inter-College Event)

    Rules :
    • The participant has to bring his/her own camera.
    • The camera should have a memory card which will be formatted before the commencement of the contest.
    • The participant has to submit 5 photographs on the theme announced on the spot.
    • No mixing/matching/morphing of photographs will be permitted.
    • Software such as Photoshop, etc. for enhancing images will not be permitted.
    • Digital images are evaluated on the basis of
      a. Impact
      b. Composition
      c. Technical quality
      d. Suitability for the specific theme
    • Theme and additional rules will be announced on the spot.
    • Vulgar, obscene or questionable photographs will lead to disqualification.
    • Time of submission will be announced on the spot.


  4. Treasure Hunt

    Rules :
    • Event will be conducted within college premises.
    • All participants must assemble at the venue 30 minutes before the start of the event.


  5. Cooking

    Rules :
    • No fire cooking.
    • Participants must carry their own materials.
    • Maximum duration and other guidelines will be announced on the spot.


  6. Dota 2 (Inter-College Event)

    Rules :
    • Each team will consist of 5 members.
    • Each game is an Elimination, Except for the final, which will be best of three matches.
    • Standard "Captain's mode" drafting for each game. (Unavailable heroes will be Terror Blade and Phantom Lancer)
    • There is no time constraint on any matches, and will be played for the entire duration. However GG can be called any time and the match may be forfeited.
    • Fixtures will be decided beforehand and time slots will be provided. Each team is expected to be present 1 hour before their match or else it may lead to disqualification.
    • All 3rd party softwares, hacks and scripts are banned. The match will be monitored closely by the host server and umpires to ensure that the games are fair.
    • There will be a coin toss to decide which team is on the Radiant and the Dire.
    • Maximum of 2 tactical pauses of 1 minute each per team.
    • No substitutions of team-mates can take place without a valid explanation and the consent of the event organizer.
    • Please get your own accesories - mouse, headsets, etc. - for a better gaming experience.


  7. Fifa 14 (Inter-College Event)

    Rules :
    • Game type: Friendlies.
      Half length: 5 minutes.
      Choice of controller: Any.
      Controls: Tactical.
      Squads: Offline.
      Game speed: Normal.
      Playing conditions: Normal, Night.
      Choice of team: League clubs for initial rounds. International for quarter and semi finals.
    • No use of custom tactics allowed. In case of a draw, game proceeds with a penalty shootout.


  8. Counter Strike 1.6 (Inter-College Event)

    Rules :
    • Each team will consist of 5 members.
    • There will be Elimination rounds followed by Quarter finals, Semi-finals and then the Finals. Elimination rounds will be played on the aa_dima2 map , Quarterfinals in de_train and Semifinals in de_dust2 and Finals in de_inferno.
    • A game will consist of two periods, consisting of five rounds each, each round will last 2.00 minutes, one period as terrorists and the other as Counter Terrorists for each team.
    • Deciding of which team plays Terrorists or Counter Terrorists will be based on a 1v1 knife round.
    • Organizers’ decision will be final in case of a discrepancy, banning or any such problem.
    • Matches will be watched closely by the host server and a few other umpires, make sure you follow the rules and play to win.
    • Substitution is allowed only once per team.
    • Cheating or using of any hacks will lead to disqualification.
    • Further rule changes, if any will be conveyed to participants on the spot, before the commencement of the game.
