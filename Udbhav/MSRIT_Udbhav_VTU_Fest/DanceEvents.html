DANCE EVENTS RULES

  1. Classical Solo

    Rules :
    • The classical dance can be from any of the approved schools of dance such as Kathak, Kathakali, Bharath Natyam, Xatriya, Manipuri, Kuchipudi, Mohiniattam, Odissi etc.
    • Participant will be allowed up to 15 minutes including time for preparation. Maximum three accompanists are permissible.
    • Judgment will be based on the qualities like taal, technique, rhythm, abhinaya or expression, costumes, footwork and general impression, etc.
    • Three copies of a brief note on the description of dance story involved in it, if any, and of the accompanying song with its meaning in Hindi or English must be submitted at the time of the event at the venue.


  2. Dance As You Like (Inter-College Event)

    Rules :
    • It is an individual event.
    • Style of dance should be purely western (freestyle, street, hip hop).
    • Prelims consist of 1 round, in which the participant has to perform for a maximum of 2+1 minutes.
    • Participants must get their own music. It should be provided in a CD/pen-drive and should be submitted 1 hour prior to the event.
    • Finals consist of 2 rounds. Rules will be announced on the spot.
    • Judgement will be based on technique and style of dance.
    • Obscenity in dress code will result in disqualification.
    • Any form of vulgarity or indecent behaviour will lead to immediate disqualification and a possible ban from further MSRIT competitions.


  3. Indian Group Dance (Inter-College Event)

    Rules :
    • Maximum 12 participants allowed per team.
    • The number of accompanists permissible is 2.
    • The dance can be either tribal/folk (Indian style or contemporary. Not classical)
    • Bollywood dance is allowed.
    • Duration of a dance should not be more than 8+2 minutes.
    • Participating teams will be responsible for removal of their sets/properties etc. immediately after the completion of performance.
    • Judgment will be based on rhythm, formation, expression, costumes, make-up, sets, and on overall effect.
    • Use of fire, explosives, dust etc. is strictly prohibited.
    • Any form of vulgarity or indecent behaviour will lead to immediate disqualification and a possible ban from further MSRIT competitions.


  4. Western Group Dance (Inter-College Event)

    Rules :
    • Team shall consist up to 16 participants.
    • Time duration is 8+2 minutes.
    • Prelims for MSRIT teams will be held one day prior to finals. The top two teams will represent MSRIT in the finals.
    • The participating teams will be responsible for the removal of their sets/properties etc. immediately after the completion of the performance.
    • Judgement will be based on choreography, costumes and overall presentation.
    • A minimum of 4 dancers and a maximum of 12 dancers on stage at any particular time.
    • Songs should be provided on a CD or pen-drive containing only songs required by that performance.
    • Props are allowed. Use of fire or other explosives strictly not allowed.
    • Same person cannot dance in more than one dance of the same category.
    • At no point of the sequence should the stage be left completely empty.
    • No profanity is to be displayed.
    • Any form of vulgarity or indecent behavior will lead to immediate disqualification and a possible ban from further MSRIT competitions.
    • In case of number of registered teams exceeding the number of slots a screening process will take place, details of which will be given to the teams.


  5. Street Dance (Inter-College Event)

    Rules :
    • A minimum of 2 and 3 participants per team for the 2 v/s 2 and 3 v/s 3 events respectively. (Non engineering crews are permitted to take part.)
    • The first round is the 'SHOWCASE' round. Here crews have to get their own music and perform an already choreographed dance. Minimum of 1 min to maximum of 5 minutes. The crews will be eliminated based on this round.
    • In the next round, random music will be played and the crews have to battle with each other. Two crews battling at once, which will be picked on a random basis.
    • If the scores are tied in the final round and judges are unable to decide, then there will be another tie breaker 'SHOWCASE' round for 1 min and the winner will be decided based on that.
