MUSIC EVENTS RULES

  1. Western Group Music - Battle of Bands (Inter-College Event)

    Rules :
    • Maximum 6 singers in a group. Maximum number of accompanists playing shall be 6.
    • Maximum time allowed for group song is 10+5 minutes which does not include setting time. The setting time for a group shall not exceed 5 minutes.
    • The prelims for the MSRIT bands will be held on the same day.
    • Judgement will be based on qualities like composition, rhythm, difficulty and general impression.
    • Any number of instruments allowed.
    • Language must be English only.
    • In case of number of registered bands exceeding the number of slots a screening process will take place, details of which will be given to the teams.


  2. Western Acoustics (Inter-College Event)

    Rules :
    • Maximum time for stage/instrument setup is 2 minutes.
    • The prelims for the MSRIT participants will be held on the 8th. The winners of the prelims will represent MSRIT in the inter event.
    • Maximum number of accompanists is 2.
    • Duration of the performance shall be between 4-6 minutes.
    • Judgement will be based on qualities like composition, rhythm, style, difficulty, fluency and general impression.


  3. Indian Light Music Solo

    Rules :
    • Time for stage or instruments setting is maximum 2mins duration .
    • Event will consist of two separate rounds – Qualifying round and Finals – which will be held on two separate days
    • Qualifying round will have a time limit of 3 minutes
    • Final round will consist of three individual rounds which will be decided by the judges. Finalists should be ready with 5 songs of preferably different genres
    • Only film songs are allowed.
    • Karaoke is not allowed for prelims as well as finals. The final rounds will be accompanied by a professional orchestra which shall be arranged.


  4. Classical Vocal Solo (Inter-College Event)

    Rules :
    • Duration of performance is 10-15 mins.
    • Maximum number of accompanists is 2.
    • Time for stage or instruments setting is maximum 5 mins.
    • Item can be presented in either Hindustani or Carnatic style.
    • Film songs are not allowed under this event.
    • Sufficient thought and care must be exercised in the choice of raga and composition.
    • Judgement will be based on qualities like taal, selection of raga, composition and general impression.


  5. Semi-Classical Vocal Solo

    Rules :
    • Time limit- 4 to 6 minutes.
    • Time for stage or instruments setting is maximum 2 mins.
    • Only non filmy songs /geeth/ghazal/bhajan/shabad and abhanges can be presented
    • A maximum of two accompanists allowed.
    • Film songs not allowed.
    • Judgement will be based on qualities like swara, taal, selection of raga, composition and overall presentation.
    • Judges decision is final and binding.


  6. Classical Instrumental Solo (Inter-College Event)

    Rules :
    • Instrument can be percussion or non percussion.
    • Duration of performance is 12-15 min.
    • Maximum time for stage/instrument setup is 5 minutes.
    • Maximum number of accompanists is 2.
    • Participants must bring their own instruments.
    • Item can be presented in either Hindustani or Carnatic style.
    • Judgement will be based on qualities like taal, selection of raga, composition and general impression.


  7. Antaakshari (Teaching Staff and Students)

    Rules :
    • A team would consist of two members
    • There would be four rounds having different modes.
    • These rounds would be declared only at the time of the game.
    • The decision of the judges would be final.


  8. Karaoke

    Rules :
    • Individual Event.
    • Duration not more than 5 mins.
    • Allowed to sing only one song.
    • Judgement will be left to the discretion of the judges.
    • Karaoke track has to be submitted to the event coordinator before 7pm on the 9th April. (Mp4 or Mp3).


  9. Antaakshari (Teaching Staff and Students)

    Rules :
    • A team would consist of two members
    • There would be four rounds having different modes.
    • These rounds would be declared only at the time of the game.
    • The decision of the judges would be final.


  10. Indian Group Music (Inter-College Event)

    Rules :
    • A team has to present 2 songs.
    • Maximum of10 participants including accompanists.
    • The songs will be taken from Indian Songs, which can be in regional languages.
    • Film songs allowed.
    • Maximum time allotted is 10 mins, without setting time.
    • Setting time not more than 4 mins.
    • Judging will be based on quality of singing and overall performance.
