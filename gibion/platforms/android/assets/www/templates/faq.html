FAQ

(as of 2/16/16)

What is GIBLIB?

GIBLIB is a surgical video library for surgeons to share and consume high quality information in an ecosystem that enhances communication and the sharing of best surgical practices with colleagues across the globe.

How do I sign up?

Visit GIBLIB.com. Then you will need to create a secure login and complete the physician verification process to complete your registration.

Who can join GIBLIB?

GIBLIB membership is reserved for physicians, fellows, residents, and medical students

How much does GIBLIB cost?

GIBLIB is free for physicians, medical students, residents, and fellows.

Why does GIBLIB need my email?

Your email address serves as your username to securely log you in to GIBLIB and will NOT be displayed on your profile or anywhere public.

Are there HIPPA risks with participating on GIBLIB?

According to HIPPA, surgical vides that are "de-identified", or do not contain identifiable patient information, can be freely distributed. If a patient can be identified, you must receive permission from the patient to post this video. You can read the relevant portions of HIPPA here: http://www.hhs.gov/ocr/privacy/hipaa/understanding/coveredentities/De-identification/guidance.html

Can international doctors sign up?

We are currently available globally but the registration process may be more cumbersome for those that are from countries other than the United States as we have to manually verify each international physicians, fellows, residents, and medical students.

Why am I getting emails from GIBLIB?

These emails are invitations sent from other members of GIBLIB to join them on GIBLIB. GIBLIB does not email unregistered medical professionals.

Can videos be downloaded to my computer?

All videos are streamed. They cannot be downloaded to your computer.

How do I find videos?

You can use the search bar or browse videos using the top navigation menu.

How do I post comments?

Next to each video is a comment box. Type your message there and click “POST”

How do I upload a video?

On the top navigation bar, click on the button that reads “Upload Now.” This will take you the Video Upload page. Feel free to drag and drop or browse your files to upload file(s). Make sure to fill out the necessary title, descriptions, specialty, procedure, and any other tags for your video. You should be taken to a confirmation page of all your uploaded videos.

If I upload a video, who retains the rights?

You retain all the rights to any videos you uploaded to GIBLIB. You may take down your videos at any time.

What does GIBLIB mean?

“GIB“ is short for Dr. John Gibbon, a heart surgeon from the mid-twentieth century best known for inventing a heart-lung machine and performing subsequent open heart surgeries, which revolutionized heart surgery. “LIB” is a techy abbreviation for “library”.

Can patients see videos?

Patients will not be able to see the videos as GIBLIB is a closed community for physicians, fellows, residents, and medical students

Why am I blocked?

For those that registered but were not verified within 3 business days may get blocked until GIBLIB is able to verify the user as either a physician, fellow, resident, and/or medical student. The other reason for a user to get blocked is if someone reported suspicious activity, spamming, or belligerent activity. If you feel that you were unfairly blocked, feel free to contact GIBLIB at contact@giblib.com and provide us with a detailed explanation or documents proving that there was an error in the blocked status.
